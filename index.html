ISF/CF

ISF (Insulin sensitivity factor) = CF (Insulin correction factor)

What is your ISF or CF?

Your ISF or CF is the amount by which 1 unit of Rapid (mealtime) insulin will lower your blood sugar.

Example:

mmol/L
1 unit of Rapid (mealtime) insulin lowers your blood sugar 2 mmol, your ISF or Cf is 1:2

mg/dL
1 unit of Rapid (mealtime) insulin lowers your blood sugar 2 mmol, your ISF or CF is 1:36

ISF/CF recommendations

mmol/L

Adult Type 2 (Adult diabetes): 2
This value may require adjusting for some Type 2 individuals.

Adult Type 1 (Juvenile diabetes): 3 or 4
This value may require adjusting for some Type 1 individuals.

mg/dL

Adult Type 2 (Adult diabetes): 36
This value may require adjusting for some Type 2 individuals.

Juvenile Type 1 (Juvenile diabetes): 54 or 72
This value may require adjusting for some Type 2 individuals.

Before meal blood sugar

Before meal blood sugar measurement should be taken within 1/2 hour of your meal.

After Meal Blood Sugar

After meal blood sugar measurement should be taken within 1/2 hour of your meal.

Rapid (mealtime) insulin

Number of units of Rapid (mealtime) insulin given for your meal.

Total Grams of carbohydrates

Total grams of carbohydrates from all sources in your meal (not including fibre).

Example:

Total Carbohydrates = 18 grams
Fiber = 2 grams
Sugar = 2 grams
Starch = 14 grams

Therefore:

18 grams of carbohydrates
- 2 grams of fiber
= 16 grams

High Fat meals

High fat content meals may produce inaccurate insulin:carbohydrate ratios.

In the vast majority of Diabetics, the same insulin to carbohydrate ratio hold true for all meals. Yet with some Diabetics, the insulin:carbohydrate ratio may vary at different meals. Initially, use the insulin to carbohydrate ratio calculator for all meals to establish correct ratio(s).
